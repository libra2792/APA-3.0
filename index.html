The mechanics below are what make Pro Achiever 3.0 different from a DIY portfolio. They're also the things most likely to be skimmed over in a brochure, so read them once, carefully.

0.325% per month of policy value, but only for the first 10 years of premium payment. From Year 11 onwards: 0%.

The Initial Investment Period anchors lock-in, bonus tiers and surrender charges. Pick to match your goal horizon.

Flexible
🌍

Curated or DIY fund selection

3 Elite Funds (institutional), 3 Mercer-Guided Portfolios, or 32 retail funds à la carte. Start simple, switch later.

Wide choice
Important: read this

During the Initial Investment Period, dividends from your funds are automatically reinvested into the policy and cannot be drawn out as cash. Cash dividend payouts are only available after the IIP ends. This is the trade-off for the welcome bonus, step-up bonus and capped supplementary charge above. If you need accessible quarterly income from day one, a single-premium ILP like Platinum Wealth Venture 2.0 may be a better fit. Speak to me about it.

The CI waiver, why it matters

Scenario: you start Pro Achiever 3.0 at age 35, S$1,000/month into IIP 20. At Year 7 (age 42), you're diagnosed with an intermediate-stage cancer. With Early Critical Protector Waiver of Premium (II), here's what happens:

step 1
Diagnosis confirmed
CI claim submitted to AIA per rider terms.
step 2
AIA waives future premiums
For your APA 3.0 plan and any eligible riders, until rider expiry (age 55 / 60 / 65, your choice).
step 3
Your premiums = AIA's premiums
The S$12,000/yr that would have come from your salary now comes from AIA. Welcome bonus, special bonus, fund growth all continue as if nothing changed.
step 4
Retirement plan intact
At age 55, policy value is the same as if you'd kept paying yourself. The CI did not derail the goal.

The rider has its own premium (paid separately, in addition to the regular premium of the basic plan), typically a few hundred dollars per year depending on age, gender, and chosen expiry age. A brokerage account or robo-advisor cannot offer this. There is no underlying insurer to take over your contributions. That is the moat.

Built-in Risk Defences
Five investor risks the plan is engineered to counter

Every investment carries risk. The question is whether the structure works with your behaviour or against it. Five documented risks, drawn in data.

i.Behavioural risk: buying high, selling low

Built into the planA fixed regular premium invests on schedule in every market. The IIP removes the panic-sell button: discipline is in the contract, not your mood.

ii.Market-timing risk: waiting for the crash
US$1,000 a month into a 60/40 portfolio, 1990-2020. Investing every month beat holding cash for 10% drawdowns by $350,000, and that assumes the market-timer had perfect discipline.
Built into the planMonthly premiums enter the market 12 times a year. You own every price along the way, so being right about timing stops mattering.

iii.Short-horizon risk
Best and worst S&P 500 rolling returns by holding period, 1973 to mid-2009. Risk concentrates in short holding periods. Stretch the horizon and even the worst outcome turns positive.
Built into the planYour IIP of 10, 15 or 20 years anchors the plan in exactly the holding periods where history favours the investor, matched to your goal date rather than your mood.

iv.Sequence-of-returns risk
Two retirees. Same $100,000, same $5,000 yearly withdrawals, same 4.0% average return. The only difference is the order in which the returns arrive.
Built into the planAfter the IIP, you can switch into a dividend-paying fund and draw the payout instead of force-selling units in a downturn, while automatic rebalancing keeps risk in check approaching drawdown. No structure removes sequence risk entirely; this one gives you levers.

v.Portfolio drift risk
A portfolio left alone drifts to a riskier mix after every rally, just in time for the next correction. Rebalancing resets it, selling high and buying low by construction.

§ 08
Is this plan right for you?
Honest fit and unfit cases
▾

This plan suits you if…

  • You have a clear long-term goal: retirement, child's tertiary education, housing upgrade in 15+ years
  • You can comfortably commit at least S$200/month (or S$2,400/year) without needing this money before the IIP ends
  • You want a structured, hands-off approach, Elite Funds or Guided Portfolios, instead of picking your own ETFs
  • You want your investment plan to continue even if a critical illness stops you from working
  • You already have an emergency fund (3-6 months expenses) and adequate hospitalisation, life and CI cover in place

This plan does NOT suit you if…

  • You may need this money before the IIP ends, surrender charges in Years 1-2 are 100%, dropping to 5% by Year 10
  • You're using this as your emergency fund or short-term savings, wrong instrument
  • You want regular cash dividend income from Day 1, dividends are reinvested during IIP. Consider PWV 2.0 instead
  • You're already maximally invested elsewhere and the additional premium would strain your monthly budget
  • You only want pure fee minimisation and have no concern about a CI derailing your plan, DIY is cheaper
